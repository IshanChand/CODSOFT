Mother Teresa

1.Birth and Early Life: She was born on August 26, 1910, in Skopje, which is now part of North Macedonia. Her family was of Albanian descent.

2.Becoming a Nun: At the age of 18, she left home to join the Sisters of Loreto, an Irish community of nuns with missions in India. She adopted the name Sister Mary Teresa after Saint Thérèse of Lisieux.

3.Mission in India: In 1929, she arrived in Calcutta (now Kolkata), India, where she taught at a school for girls. She later served as the school's principal.

4.Call to Serve the Poor: In 1946, Mother Teresa experienced what she referred to as a "call within a call." She believed that she received a divine message to work among the poorest of the poor.

5.Founding the Missionaries of Charity: In 1950, Mother Teresa founded the Missionaries of Charity, a religious congregation dedicated to helping those in need. The organization provided assistance to the sick, dying, orphaned, and homeless.

6.Humble Lifestyle: Mother Teresa lived a simple and austere life. She and her fellow sisters wore simple white saris with blue borders, which became a symbol of their mission.

7.Nobel Peace Prize: Mother Teresa received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979 for her compassionate work with the destitute and dying. She used the prize money to fund her various charitable efforts.

8.Global Impact: Mother Teresa's work inspired people around the world to engage in charitable activities and care for the less fortunate. Her influence extended beyond religious boundaries.
